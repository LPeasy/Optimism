## Email 5: Data Boundaries And Evidence

Send: day 8  
Goal: teach control layer and address security/reliability concerns  
Primary CTA: book setup discussion

Subject options:

1. The two AI rules most teams skip
2. Before the workflow runs, define these boundaries
3. Data boundary first. Output second.

Preview text:

Most AI risk starts before the prompt is written.

Body:

Hi {{first_name}},

Two controls prevent a lot of AI pilot trouble:

1. Data boundaries.
2. Source evidence.

Data boundaries answer:

- What inputs are approved?
- What data is prohibited?
- Which systems may the workflow touch?
- What must stay human-only?
- How long are files and outputs retained?

Source evidence answers:

- Which claims need support?
- What source ID backs each material point?
- Which assumptions are unverified?
- What must a reviewer check before approval?

Without these two controls, AI outputs can look polished while still being unsupported, risky, or impossible to audit.

Optimism's AI Governance Setup creates those boundaries before implementation.
