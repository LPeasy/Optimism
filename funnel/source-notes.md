# Source Notes

## Email Funnel Best Practices

HubSpot lead-nurturing guidance emphasizes the right message, timing, audience, and next step. It also warns against wall-of-copy emails, missing CTAs, and pushing a conversion before the reader understands the offer.

Source: https://blog.hubspot.com/blog/tabid/6307/bid/5917/5-Best-Practices-for-Lead-Nurturing-Emails.aspx

HubSpot B2B email guidance recommends one primary CTA per email, action-oriented copy, mobile-readable formatting, and revenue-linked funnel math.

Source: https://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/b2b-email-marketing

Mailchimp benchmark data provides a rough baseline for open and click rates. Its page reports business and finance average open rate of 31.35% and click rate of 2.78%, with all-user averages of 35.63% and 2.62%. Mailchimp notes open-rate accuracy can be affected by Apple Mail Privacy Protection.

Source: https://mailchimp.com/resources/email-marketing-benchmarks/

FTC CAN-SPAM guidance requires accurate header information, non-deceptive subject lines, ad identification where required, valid postal address, clear opt-out, prompt opt-out honoring, and vendor monitoring.

Source: https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/can-spam-act-compliance-guide-business

Google sender guidelines require email authentication and, for bulk/subscription traffic, easy unsubscribe practices including one-click unsubscribe where applicable.

Source: https://support.google.com/mail/answer/81126

## AI Efficiency And Controlled Implementation Evidence

NBER research by Brynjolfsson, Li, and Raymond found that AI assistance increased customer-support productivity by about 14% on average.

Source: https://www.nber.org/papers/w31161

Harvard Business School AI Institute summarizes BCG field research finding that, for tasks within the AI frontier, GPT-4 increased speed by more than 25%, human-rated performance by more than 40%, and task completion by more than 12%.

Source: https://d3.harvard.edu/navigating-the-jagged-technological-frontier/

McKinsey's 2025 AI survey reports broad AI use, growing agent experimentation, use-case-level cost and revenue benefits, and uneven enterprise-level EBIT impact. It identifies workflow redesign as a key success factor.

Source: https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai

The U.S. Census Bureau reports that overall business AI use hovered around 17% to 20% from December 2025 to May 2026, with larger firms adopting at higher rates.

Source: https://www.census.gov/library/stories/2026/05/ai-use-businesses.html

Microsoft's 2026 Work Trend Index emphasizes agent workflows, human handoffs, quality standards, and repeatable operating patterns.

Source: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/worklab/work-trend-index/agents-human-agency-and-the-opportunity-for-every-organization

NIST's AI Risk Management Framework supports AI governance, risk management, measurement, and ongoing management. This supports Optimism's control-first framing.

Source: https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework
