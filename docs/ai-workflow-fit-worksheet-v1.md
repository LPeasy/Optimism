# AI Workflow Intake Worksheet v1

This is an optional prep worksheet. It helps a respondent describe their business, name the messy workflow they want to improve, list the tools already in use, and identify where AI might help without taking control away from people.

The website form is the canonical intake record. This worksheet is for call prep, internal discussion, or offline note-taking.

## How To Use

Fill this out before a Workflow Fit Call if you want to think through the workflow in more detail. Rough answers are enough. The point is to help the respondent see their own need: where work is slow, where tools do not connect, where manual handoffs create rework, and where AI assistance may or may not be useful.

## Worksheet Sections

### Business Snapshot

- Company or organization
- Respondent role
- What the business or team does
- Main customer, stakeholder, or internal user served

### Tools And AI Reality

- Current tools: email, calendar, spreadsheet, CRM, forms, documents, job software, accounting, or other tools
- Current AI stage: not using AI, experimenting, regular use, blocked by risk, or hard to manage
- Where AI is already creating questions, delay, rework, or risk
- What has made the team cautious or curious

### Workflow Opportunity

- One recurring workflow that could be improved
- Where that workflow wastes time, creates errors, slows sales, weakens reporting, or increases risk
- What better would look like in plain business terms

### System Design Signals

- Workflow: the trigger, owner, output, and review point are clear or unclear
- Tools: the current apps, spreadsheets, inboxes, calendars, and documents are clear or unclear
- Handoffs: who receives what, when, and with what context is clear or unclear
- Data: approved inputs, sensitive data, access, and retention are clear or unclear
- AI fit: drafting, summarizing, classifying, extracting, or checking would clearly help or would distract
- Support: training, documentation, stop rules, and change path are clear or unclear

### Next Step Fit

- Desired outcome: save time, improve quality, support revenue, reduce risk, speed reporting, or clarify fit
- Timeline: now, next 30 days, next 90 days, later, or unsure
- Budget readiness: ready for a $5k+ build, need a smaller blueprint first, or exploring fit
- What would make a 20-minute Workflow Fit Call worth it

## Source Controls

This v1 worksheet uses the Optimism landing-page categories: Workflow, Tools, Handoffs, Data, AI Fit, and Support; plus governance-kit controls for sensitive data, tool misuse, overreliance, weak evidence, drift, access creep, and accountability.
