# AI Workflow Fit Worksheet v1

This is an optional prep worksheet. It helps a respondent describe their business, name their current AI questions, and identify the workflow where a controlled AI pilot might be useful.

The website form is the canonical intake record. This worksheet is for call prep, internal discussion, or offline note-taking.

## How To Use

Fill this out before a Workflow Fit Call if you want to think through the workflow in more detail. Rough answers are enough. The point is to help the respondent see their own need: where work is slow, where AI is already leaking in, where risk shows up, and what a useful first pilot would improve.

## Worksheet Sections

### Business Snapshot

- Company or organization
- Respondent role
- What the business or team does
- Main customer, stakeholder, or internal user served

### Current AI Reality

- Current AI stage: not using AI, experimenting, regular use, blocked by risk, or hard to manage
- Where AI is already creating questions, delay, rework, or risk
- What has made the team cautious or curious

### Workflow Opportunity

- One recurring workflow that could be improved
- Where that workflow wastes time, creates errors, slows sales, weakens reporting, or increases risk
- What better would look like in plain business terms

### Need And Risk Signals

- Scope: the workflow, output, owner, and review point are clear or unclear
- Data: approved inputs, sensitive data, and retention rules are clear or unclear
- Tools: allowed tools, access limits, and blocked actions are clear or unclear
- Evidence: source support, assumptions, and exceptions are clear or unclear
- Approval: human approval before business action is clear or unclear
- Recovery: stop conditions, incidents, and rollback routes are clear or unclear

### Next Step Fit

- Desired outcome: save time, improve quality, support revenue, reduce risk, speed reporting, or clarify fit
- Timeline: now, next 30 days, next 90 days, later, or unsure
- What would make a 20-minute Workflow Fit Call worth it

## Source Controls

This v1 worksheet uses the Optimism landing-page categories: Scope, Data, Tools, Evidence, Approval, and Recovery; plus governance-kit controls for prompt injection, sensitive data, tool misuse, overreliance, weak evidence, drift, access creep, and accountability.
